Overview
This is a take-home round for the Backend Engineer position at HGN. You'll get a small,
scoped problem close to what our platform actually deals with, and we're mainly checking

There's no single correct answer here. We want to see your reasoning and how you handle a

The Problem
HGN tracks trekkers and groups using GPS/satellite tracking devices, and coordinates SOS
response when something goes wrong on the trail.
Build a small backend service for SOS alert intake and dispatch coordination that handles
the following.
Core Requirements
1. Device sends an alert
A tracking device sends an SOS signal with a device ID, GPS coordinates, and a timestamp.
Record it as an alert.
2. One device, multiple orders over time
A device can be tied to more than one active order or booking (reused across treks,
reassigned, etc). Your API needs to ﬁgure out which order an incoming SOS belongs to
when the device isn't cleanly one-to-one with a single order. Don't assume it always is.
3. One device, multiple people in a group
A group booking might share a single device across several trekkers instead of giving
everyone their own. Your data model and API should handle an alert from a shared device
making sense at the group level.
4. Deduplication
Devices on bad satellite connectivity sometimes retransmit the same SOS multiple times. A
retransmission close in time shouldn't create a second alert. A genuinely new SOS should.
You decide what counts as "close in time," just explain why you picked that number.
5. Concurrency-safe claiming
A coordinator claims or acknowledges an alert to start responding. Two coordinators
should never both end up claiming the same alert. This needs to actually hold up under

System design: how the pieces ﬁt together (services, layers, how a request ﬂows from
intake to dispatch), and what you'd change if this had to scale to hundreds of devices

Data model: how you represented devices, orders, groups, and alerts, and why you

why, given the time you had.

Ground this in your actual code: real table names, real endpoints, and any problems you
